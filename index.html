Bird in college 2.0 UGA

What We Want

The reimplementation of Bird electric scooters throughout the University of Georgia campus in cooperation with administration and campus police in an effort to benefit the general student body and accomplish university sustainability goals

The Full Story

Aug. 14th...

Bird introduced hundreds of electric scooters throughout campus, downtown Athens, and the surrounding area. This introduction coincided with similar introductions on 149 other college campuses.

Following Week...

Students flocked to the new transportation service, utilizing the scooters to get to class, get food, and just move about their day and they took to social media to share their praise.

The Conflict...

Students were unaware of the rules regarding where you can and cannot park and drive the vehicles. As a result, campus administration and campus police began impounding scooters and issuing citations to students.

President Morehead's 2017 University Review

Quote Result
"allow UGA to approach complex problems holistically" The university failed to explore how the new technology could have benefited the whole community
"We will never be satisfied with marinating the status quo at this university" By removing scooters within a week of deployment the university failed to challenge status quo in any way
"Because we are a responsive university, we continue to focus resources and energy on solving emerging challenges in communities" The university reacted harshly to the scooters rather than working to respond to the unique problem and find a dynamic solution

2015 Sustainability Packet Review

UGA Goal Result
Increase by 20% the number of faculty, staff, and students who bus, bike, or walk to campus; increase the number of Alternative Transportation Opportunities. The university could have chosen to integrate Bird scooters as a "Alternative Transportation" method to the current three types of active transportation to increase usage
Continue to develop a connected, pedestrian-centered campus. The university failed to see how Bird scooters contribute to unique system connecting the Athens community
Establish car share and tide share opportunities for students, faculty and staff. The university should have taken advantage of Birds systems and worked with Bird to develop these scooters as an alternative to the planned ride sharing program

Plan of Action

  1. Contact Bird and return any currently impounded scooters
  2. Establish a relationship with Bird to implement a plan for proper education on usage and safety
  3. Work with campus organizations to deploy the education plan and properly inform students on Bird usage
  4. Allow Bird to slowly reintroduce the electric scooters throughout campus in a phased approach
  5. Continue enforcing rules and regulations regarding usage
  6. Make adaptations to the education plan based on the initial success of the phased introduction
  7. Fully reintroduce Bird electric scooters throughout campus
